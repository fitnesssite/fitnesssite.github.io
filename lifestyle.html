FAQ

Every hard-working adult is stressed and sleep deprived. We get it - It just happens. But, not managing your stress and neglecting your sleep habits may be wreaking havoc on your body. The trick is to make sure you have a good balance and recognize when you’re over-exerting your body so that you can plan some relaxing time.

Stress

Stress can literally eat away at your body, both mentally and physically. When you’re stressed out, your brain releases a stress hormone, like adrenaline and cortisol, into your blood. These are also known as “fight or flight” response hormones, and when too much of these hormones stay in your system unchecked, it can lead to some pretty serious issues. Your body does not know the difference between what kind of stress you are reacting to. So whether you’re giving a big presentation at the office or running away from a grizzly bear, your brain will release the same hormones to keep your body alert.

Sleep

Getting enough sleep at night is a great way to help lower your stress levels - everyone LOVES a refreshing, full 8+ hour, drool-on-your-pillow night of sleep! When you sleep your brain is working to make support your physical health, brain functionality, and safety. Studies show that depriving your brain and body of sleep (consistently) can cause mood swings, give you a lack of motivation, and proves to give you trouble with decision-making. Not only does sleep help you maintain a proper balance of hormones in your body, but it also gives your body a chance to repair your heart, muscles, joints, and brain. Your immune system also relies on getting enough sleep in order to stay healthy. Each adult is different when it comes to the amount of sleep. The average adult is built to stay awake for 16 hours and sleep for 8. However some adults can function perfectly on 6 hours of sleep, while others need 10 hours. It’s safe to say anywhere between 6-8 hours is a “good amount” to get each night.
